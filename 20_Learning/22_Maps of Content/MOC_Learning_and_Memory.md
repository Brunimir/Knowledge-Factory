### The Capacity Constraint - why the problem exists at all
- [[The_Magical_Number_Seven]] - establishes the hard ceiling: working memory holds ~7 items, which means compression is not optional, it is biological necessity
- [[Cluster_Processing]] - reveals the architecture beneath the constraint
- [[Information_Overload_Tax]] - the applied consequence of the seven-item ceiling: when the incoming signal exceeds working memory capacity, processing stops and nothing is retained: the brain processes in spatial clusters, not word-by-word, which is why unstructured information is discarded
### The Encoding Phase - building the initial trace
- [[Focused_Mode]] - the cognitive state required to build a chunk; undivided attention is the prerequisite for any encoding to occur
- [[Chunking]] - the compression act itself; binding discrete items into a single retrievable packet that bypasses the seven-item ceiling
- [[Process_vs_Product]] - the procrastination antidote; how to actually enter Focused Mode when the brain resists the effort of encoding
### The Consolidation Phase - making the trace permanent
- [[Sleep_and_Learning]] - the non-negotiable biological maintenance cycle; without sleep, encoded traces are not consolidated into long-term memory
- [[Diffuse_Mode]] - the relaxed state where distant concepts connect; the cognitive mortar that sets the bricks laid during focused sessions
### The Retrieval Phase - strengthening through use
- [[Active_Recall]] - the act of pulling information out rather than putting it in; the single most effective method for deepening neural traces
- [[Spaced_Repetition]] - the timing system for retrieval; revisiting at increasing intervals prevents metabolic decay of the memory trace
- [[Interleaving]] - the challenge mechanism; mixing problem types forces the brain to select the right strategy, not just execute a known one - producing flexible rather than brittle knowledge

## Key Tensions
**Focused Mode vs. Diffuse Mode - you cannot do both at once.** The brain cannot encode new technical detail and make creative connections simultaneously. Grinding in focused mode when stuck does not produce diffuse-mode breakthroughs; it deepens the block. The tension is practical: knowing *when to stop* is as important as knowing how to focus.

**Active Recall vs. the Illusion of Competence.** Re-reading feels productive because it is comfortable. Active recall feels difficult because it is. The notes that are hardest to retrieve are the ones most in need of retrieval - but the discomfort makes learners avoid the technique that would help them most.

**Chunking vs. Premature Compression.** Chunks must be built on genuine understanding or they become hollow - usable in familiar contexts but brittle under novel conditions. The tension: compression is necessary for expertise, but compression without understanding produces confident incompetence.

**Spaced Repetition vs. Deadline Reality.** The optimal spacing intervals for long-term retention are incompatible with exam-week cramming. The method that works best for durable knowledge is structurally incompatible with how most formal learning is assessed.

**Sleep vs. Productivity Culture.** Sleep is the most important learning intervention in this MOC, yet it is the one most systematically sacrificed by high performers. Cortisol elevation from overwork actively suppresses the consolidation that sleep provides - connecting directly to [[MOC_Biology_of_Performance]].
