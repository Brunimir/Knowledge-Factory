## Core Idea (Summary)
Focused mode is the brain's spotlight state — directing full attention at a specific problem to follow logical steps, activate familiar patterns, or deliberately build new ones. It is like a high-density pinball machine where the bumpers are close together, channelling the ball into known pathways. It is the prerequisite for encoding; nothing gets chunked without it.

## Mechanism (Key details)
- Located primarily in the prefrontal cortex; uses sequential, step-by-step reasoning to link ideas.
- Requires undivided attention — divided attention wastes the limited slots of working memory and prevents encoding from reaching the depth needed for chunking.
- Used either to activate known neural patterns or deliberately create new ones through practice.
- The Einstellung effect: when the same approach keeps failing, focused mode deepens the block rather than breaking it — the existing pattern crowds out the novel one.
- **Boundary condition:** Focused mode cannot produce creative or distant-connection insights; that is [[Diffuse_Mode]]'s function. Grinding in focused mode when stuck does not produce breakthroughs — it typically deepens the block. The signal to switch is repeated failure with the same approach.

## Application
- **When to use:** When learning the technical details of a new concept, solving a specific problem, or performing tasks requiring high precision and sequential logic.
- **When NOT to use:** When stuck — when the same approach keeps failing (Einstellung block), exit focused mode deliberately. Take a walk, shower, or sleep. Do not grind. Forcing more focus when stuck deepens the block.
