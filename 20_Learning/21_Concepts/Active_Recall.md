## Core Idea (Summary)
Active recall is the practice of pulling information out of your head rather than putting more in. Testing your memory is the training, not a measure of it — like building muscle by lifting, not by watching someone else lift.

## Mechanism (Key details)
- Retrieval strengthens the neural pathway each time it is used; re-reading the same page does not — the trace is activated but not deepened.
- Exposes the illusion of competence: re-reading feels productive because the text is familiar; active recall reveals what you actually own versus what you merely recognise.
- Most effective when practised in varied physical environments, making the memory independent of location cues.
- **Boundary condition:** Active recall requires at least a minimal conceptual hook before it works. Trying to recall material you have never encountered produces nothing; you need a first pass of focused encoding before retrieval practice can deepen it.

## Application
- **When to use:** As the primary study method for any material that must be retrievable months later — close the book after a page and reconstruct the key points before reading on.
- **When NOT to use:** Do not use it as a first encounter with genuinely new material; a basic understanding must exist before retrieval practice can operate on it.
