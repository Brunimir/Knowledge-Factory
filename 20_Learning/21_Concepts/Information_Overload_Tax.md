## Core Idea (Summary)
If you give people too much data at once, their brains stop working correctly and they can't remember anything you said. It is like trying to fill a small cup with a fire hose; most of the water just splashes away and is wasted.
- **Example:** Breaking a long section into "bite-sized pieces" so the audience can actually assimilate the points.

## Mechanism (Key details)
- **Reduced Working Memory:** The brain has a limited "buffer" for new info.
- **Cognitive Processing:** Overloading stops the audience from being able to link your points together. 
- **Bite-Sized Rule:** Content should be delivered in small, manageable chunks.
- **Visual Clarity:** Slides should have one message each to prevent eye-strain and confusion.

## Application
- **When to use:** Use this when presenting complex technical or financial data to ensure key takeaways aren't lost.
- **When NOT to use:** Do not over-simplify to the point of removing necessary context if the audience is already expert-level and expects depth.
