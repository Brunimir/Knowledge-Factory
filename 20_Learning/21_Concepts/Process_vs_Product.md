## Core Idea (Summary)
To beat procrastination, you should focus on the time you spend working (the process) rather than the final goal (the product). It is like focusing on the act of walking one step at a time rather than staring at the mountaintop, which makes the journey feel less overwhelming.
- **Example:** Saying "I will work for 25 minutes" (Process) instead of saying "I must finish this 10-page report today" (Product).

## Mechanism (Key details)
- "Products" trigger the pain centres of the brain, leading to the "poisonous habit" of procrastination.
- "Processes" relate to simple, non-judgmental habits that bypass the brain's resistance.
- Utilizing the Pomodoro Technique (25 minutes of focus) is the primary tool for focusing on process.
- This shift allows you to back away from judging your progress and simply put forth your best effort.

## Application
- **When to use:** Use whenever you feel resistance or dread toward starting a task.
- **When NOT to use:** Not as helpful for very short, 2-minute tasks that don't cause significant mental friction.
